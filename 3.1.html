Bipolar I Disorder

Characterized by at least one manic episode, which may be preceded or followed by hypomanic or depressive episodes.

Etiology (Biopsychosocial Model)

Biological: Strong genetic basis (heritability ~80%), dopamine and circadian rhythm dysregulation.

Psychological: Goal dysregulation, personality traits (e.g., high impulsivity).

Social: Stressful life events, family conflict, sleep disruption.

Symptoms & Diagnosis (Mnemonic: DIG FAST)

  • D - Distractibility
  • I - Indiscretion (risk-taking)
  • G - Grandiosity
  • F - Flight of ideas
  • A - Activity increase
  • S - Sleep deficit (↓ need for sleep)
  • T - Talkativeness (pressured speech)

Duration: ≥1 week or requiring hospitalization.

Treatment

Mood stabilizers: Lithium, valproate.

Atypical antipsychotics: e.g., olanzapine.

Psychosocial: Psychoeducation, CBT, family-focused therapy.

Info

DSM-5-TR Code

F31.9 (use specifiers for episode types)

Prevalence

~1.5% 12-month prevalence; equal among males and females.

Personal Remarks

The highs of mania often feel euphoric—but the crash can be devastating. Stabilization allows individuals to reclaim their rhythm and relationships.
